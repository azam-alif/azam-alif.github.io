The SUST LGBTQ+ Support Committee works to ensure every student’s right to study with dignity and safety. We provide confidential reporting, response protocols, training for allies, and clear conduct standards.

Our Mandate

  • Protect students from harassment, bullying, and discrimination.
  • Offer confidential advice and referrals (counseling, legal, medical).
  • Train faculty, staff, and student leaders on inclusive practices.
  • Monitor campus climate and recommend policy improvements.

Report an Incident (Confidential)

If you witness or experience harassment, threats, outing, or discrimination, use any channel below:

  1. Email (confidential): committee@loveislove.org
  2. Anonymous form: ../contact.html#anonymous
  3. Emergency: call campus security and a trusted faculty advisor.

Confidentiality: We do not share names or details beyond the minimum required to ensure safety, and only with your informed consent unless there is an immediate risk of harm.

Code of Conduct (Applies to Everyone)

  • No harassment, slurs, threats, or non-consensual disclosure (“outing”).
  • Respect names, pronouns, and privacy in all settings.
  • Report observed misconduct promptly.
  • Consequences may include warnings, restorative actions, or formal discipline under university policy.

Resources & Support

  • Counseling: Student Wellness Center — confidential appointments.
  • Legal Aid: University Legal Clinic (student services wing).
  • Medical: On-campus clinic; request a confidential visit.
  • Ally Training: Monthly 90-minute workshop for faculty, staff, and students. Sign up.
  • Peer Support: Weekly moderated circle — respectful, judgment-free.

Safety Tips

  • Use private channels for sensitive communication; avoid shared devices.
  • If you feel unsafe, leave the area and contact security or a trusted adult.
  • Document incidents (time, place, witnesses) if safe to do so.

Quick FAQ

Is this page monitored?

Yes, by the committee. We respond to reports and keep aggregate, non-identifying statistics to improve policy.

Will I be outed if I report?

No. We do not disclose your identity without consent unless there is imminent risk of serious harm.

Can allies join?

Yes. See Ally Training above.
